BI Inc. Senior Project

For my senior project, my team and I worked with a company called BI Incorporated, whos primary product is an ankle monitor for defendants awaiting pretrial. Currently their device uses a naive approach to check for collisions between itself and predetermined boundaries on a map. Our team implemented a novel approach to this checking using a quadtree data structure and searching algorithm. Similar to a classic binary search tree, this structure uses quaternary branching to more efficiently store and search through a problem space.

The above graphic illustrates the efficiency of the quadtree over the naive approach to searching. Our approach will save the device battery by reducing the amount of operations done by the onboard CPU.
